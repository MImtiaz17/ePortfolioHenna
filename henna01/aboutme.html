About Me

I have always loved art in many forms: drawing, painting, and much more. Drawing especially is an artform I naturally gravitate towards. But Henna is an artform I had discoverd when I was a teen. Technically, I discovered it every Eid since as I used to go out and get my Henna done as a kid. But I didnt know what that Henna paste was or how to even do it. I was intimidated to say the least.But one day, my aunt gifted me a Henna cone straight from Pakistan and my interest grew. I started practicing on glassware, then myself, then my family, and finally I was confident to apply Henna on other people.

I used my Final Project as an oppertunity to create an ePortfolio to track my jounrey with Henna. At first, I didn't know what a Henna ePortfolio would look like so I looked at both hennabydivya and gopi-henna. However, both of these ePortfolios were also selling a service of some kind, which is'nt what I'm doing. I also don't have a lot of photos since I had only recently (last 2 years) gotten confident with Henna. So this ePortfolio will be a begiiners Portfolio. Hopefully I can continue to improve and you too can get inspired to try out Henna yourself! Some tips I would like to include are...

  1. Start practicing on glassware: this way you can make mistakes more freely, thus making you improve much faster.
  2. Try copying simple designs first: before you freehand or go overbord, I suggest you start copying beginner friendly designs until you can do them easily. Then you can move on to the more harder designs and eventually freehand.
  3. PRACTICE PRACTICE PRACTICE: this is the #1 tip in getting better at not just Henna but any subject you want to learn. Practice makes perfect afterall.
